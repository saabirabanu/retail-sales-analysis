# retail-sales-analysis
End-to-end retail data analysis project (500K+ records) using Excel, SQL, and Tableau to derive insights on sales trends, customer segmentation, and revenue optimization.

## Project Overview
## 📊 Retail Sales Analysis (Capstone Project)

## Executive Summary
Conducted end-to-end analysis on 500,000+ retail transactions (2009–2011) to identify revenue drivers, customer purchasing behavior, product performance, and geographical trends. The analysis delivered measurable insights to support revenue optimization and strategic inventory planning.

## Business Objectives
- Identify high-revenue products, customers, and countries
- Analyze seasonal and time-based sales patterns
- Measure profitability and profit margins
- Support inventory and operational decision-making

## Dataset Overview
- 500,000+ transaction records
- Time Period: 2009–2011
- Key Fields: Invoice, Stock Code, Description, Quantity, Invoice Date-Time, Customer ID, Country, Unit Price

## Tools & Technologies
- Excel – Data Cleaning & Preprocessing
- SQL – KPI Computation & Analytical Queries
- Tableau – Dashboard Development & Visualization

## Key Performance Indicators
- Total Revenue: ₹6.1 Billion
- Total Quantity Sold: 12.1 Million Units
- Total Orders: 485,000+
- Average Order Value (AOV): ₹359,000
- Total Profit: ₹72.7 Million
- Average Profit Margin: 30%
- Total Customers: 6,000+

## Analytical Approach
- Time Series Analysis (Year, Month, Week, Day, Hour)
- Geographical Sales Distribution Analysis
- Product Performance Evaluation
- Customer Revenue & Order Frequency Analysis
- Inventory Trend Assessment

## Key Business Insights
- Peak sales observed in July, particularly Week 21
- Highest revenue generated from the United Kingdom
- Thursday evenings recorded maximum sales activity
- Identified top revenue-generating products and high-margin SKUs
- Recognized high-value customers contributing significantly to revenue

## Strategic Recommendations
- Increase inventory allocation during peak sales periods
- Focus marketing efforts on high-margin products
- Optimize low-performing product inventory
- Expand operations in high-performing geographic regions
- Implement structured customer loyalty programs
